Changing the Guard takes place at Buckingham Palace Forecourt every Monday, Wednesday, Friday and Sunday. It starts at 10:45am and lasts for about 45 minutes.
Soldiers gather at St James's Palace and Wellington Barracks from 10:00am and march to Buckingham Palace accompanied by music.

If you take the train to London Victoria, you can walk to Buckingham Palace in just 10 minutes. Alternatively, take the tube to St James's Park station, on the Circle (yellow) and District (green) lines. This is the closest tube station to Buckingham Palace. The best stop to get off the London Big Bus to visit Kensington Palace is the Kensington Palace stop (34) on the Blue Route. This stop is located just a short walk from the palace entrance. The Blue Route runs from East to West, so you can hop on the bus at any stop along the route and get off at Gloucester Road.

Monday, 7/3, 2023

Travel to Windsor is from Paddington only when included with the London Pass. Simply show your London pass to the ticket controller at the ticket barrier at Paddington station when boarding a train for Slough - they will let you through. Once at Slough, you will need to change trains to Windsor & Eton Central.
We can take District line from Turnhan Green to Ealing Broadway (4 stops), then Elizabeth Line (6 stops) to Slougth, then Great Western Railway to Windsor & Eton Central Station.

We also can take Bus 190 to Richmond. There are a few ways to get from Richmond to Windsor Castle. By train The fastest and most convenient way is to take the train. There is a direct train from Richmond Station to Windsor & Eton Riverside Station, which takes about 33 minutes. Trains depart every hour, and the first train leaves at 5:43am and the last train leaves at 11:43pm. The cost of a single ticket is £7.50 for adults and £4.80 for children. By bus There are also a few bus routes that go from Richmond to Windsor Castle. The quickest bus is the 701, which takes about 45 minutes. Buses depart every 30 minutes, and the first bus leaves at 5:30am and the last bus leaves at 10:30pm. The cost of a single ticket is £4.50 for adults and £3 for children.

Windsor Castle It is closed on Tuesday and Wednesdays.

St Paul Cathedral It opens Monday to Saturday at 8:30 (10:00 for Wednesday).
Take the District Line to Blackfriars, then walk to ST Paul's Cathedral.

Pay nothing at the gate. There is no need to pre-book, simply show your pass upon entry. View the Nave, the Dome, High Altar, Grand Organ and Crypt. Your pass grants you access to the cathedral, plus a free audio tour.

The Cathedral is located in central London, within easy walking distance of several London Underground and mainline train stations including:
270 metres (3 minutes) from St Paul’s Underground station.
300 metres (4 minutes) from City Thameslink station.
500 metres (7 minutes) from Blackfriars Underground and train station.

Take the Thames River Boat Cruise (Run by City Cruises) to Greenwich. The boats stop at four different piers: Westminster, London Eye, Tower and Greenwich. The boats leave each pier around every 40 minutes. The first boats of the day leave Westminster and Tower piers at 10am. The first boat from London Eye Pier leaves at 10.10am, while the first from Greenwich leaves at 10.30am. The last boat leaving Greenwich departs at 6.30pm. The last one going all the way from Westminster to Greenwich departs at 16.40. Later services from Westminster only go as far as Tower, with the last service departing Westminster at 7.20pm. The full length of the cruise, heading from Westminster to Greenwich piers, takes 70 minutes one way, 180 minutes return. From Westminster to Tower Pier, it’s 40 minutes one way, 80 minutes return. From Tower to Greenwich, it’s 30 minutes one way, 80 minutes return.
